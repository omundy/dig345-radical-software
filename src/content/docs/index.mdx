## 1-2 Introduction 

<CustomAside type="discussion" oneline="true">First day activity 
<IconTime>:20 min</IconTime></CustomAside>



{/* 
Give them a simple course-related task to complete, and offer a brief injunction before they start: 
*/}

##### Part 1

1. **Pair up** (ideally with someone you haven't met previously) and introduce yourself.
1. **Discuss** How do you define "[radical](https://www.oed.com/dictionary/radical_adj?tl=true) software"? Find an example and explain why it fits.
1. **Write** a short list of steps (as pseudocode) for someone who might act out the tasks of a simple example of radical software. A human should be able to perform the steps (as if testing them) in less than 5 minutes. Your software need not be intentionally good or bad‚Äîjust functional. There are no wrong answers!

#### Part 2

1. **Join** with another pair to form a group of 2 pairs.
1. **Perform** the pseudocode steps from the other pair while they watch and take notes. You can ask for guidance to keep moving, but try to interpret as best as you can on your own. 
1. **Switch** and perform the other group's "software".
1. **Make** notes to share with the class 
   1. Did the other pair follow the steps as you "coded" them? What went wrong (or right)?
   1. How do the outcomes change or depend based on who codes the software and their motivations?
   1. What is "radical software?"
