### DevLog 5-3  

#### Create an interactive sound experience

1. Review the [Sound & Music page](/dig345-radical-software/tutorials/sound-music) to learn Tone.js and Strudel.
1. Complete the Strudel [First Sounds](https://strudel.cc/workshop/first-sounds/) tutorial. Post a link to your finished REPL ✏️ here.
1. Complete the Strudel [First Notes](https://strudel.cc/workshop/first-notes/) tutorial. Post a link to your finished REPL ✏️ here.
1. Complete the Strudel [First Effects](https://strudel.cc/workshop/first-effects/) tutorial. Post a link to your finished REPL ✏️ here. 
1. [Music Mouse Explained | Laurie Spiegel on Algorithmic Composition (1987 Archive)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aJBDHxyLEHg) (27:38). Post a reading response in your DevLog per information in the [Assignments](./#assignments).
1. Complete all five parts of the [Getting Started with Tone.js | Web Audio Tutorial - Pts 1-5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ftGuDvm5zDQ&list=PLMPgoZdlPumfkA6iYubXHyrRtdIg9T3Na) (3:31, 6:37, 10:20, 5:59, 9:28). Post a link to your finished project online ✏️ here.
1. Explore the inspiring examples below. 
1. Design and code an "interactive audio experience" that makes use of Tone.js. Consider using shapes, color, and other visuals in addition to sounds. This need not be fancy or polished, since you have less than a week to complete it. Try to have fun creating.
1. Post designs mockups (pencil/paper, Figma, etc.) here. ✏️
1. Post links to your project, a video, and a 100-150 word statement about the concept and technology of your interactive audio experience ✏️ here.


Optional Challenge: Use the Strudel [device motion](https://strudel.cc/learn/devicemotion/) and [input devices](https://strudel.cc/learn/input-devices/) to create your audio experience.
